Lesson 3

Learning Guide

Design before you build. Spend 15 minutes mapping your 5-part workflow on paper. This saves an hour of confused building later.

Test with real data. Don't test with examples. Use actual inputs from your work. Real data surfaces real problems immediately.

Start with the trigger. If the trigger doesn't fire, nothing else happens. Get this right first before moving to any other step.

Embrace the errors. When something breaks, click the error. Read it. Most fixes are obvious once you understand what went wrong.

Rules of the Road

  • Design your 5-part workflow before you touch any automation platform.
  • Test your trigger first. If it doesn't fire, fix it before moving forward.
  • Use real data from your work, not fake examples.
  • Log everything. Your logs become the system's memory.
  • A workflow that works 90% of the time is ship-ready. Perfect is the enemy of shipped.
  • When something breaks, diagnose and fix, don't start over.
Pro Tip

Before you log into any automation platform, spend 15 minutes writing down your complete 5-part workflow. Describe each step in 2-3 sentences. What triggers it? What data comes in? What does the AI do? Where does output go? What gets logged? This clarity saves you hours of confused building.

Brief 1 Teaching

Why You're Still the Bottleneck

In Mission 2, you built a Packaged AI tool. It works. You press the button, it generates output, and the quality is good. But then what happens?

You become the connector. You manually paste input. You wait for output. You copy it. You switch to email or Slack or a document. You paste it. You check the formatting. You might add context. You send it. You log it somewhere, or you don't, and now there's no record. You repeat this every single day.

The AI is fast. Three seconds per response. You are slow. Five minutes of manual work per response. When you add a second workflow, you're now the bottleneck for both. When you add a third, three. The tool scales. You don't.

This is called the 95 percent problem. MIT Sloan research shows that 95 percent of organizations investing in AI get zero return. Not because the AI doesn't work. Because they bolt AI onto broken processes and automate chaos. Process excellence comes before AI, every single time.

The solution isn't a better AI tool. It's a workflow. A system where data flows automatically, AI processes it, and output lands where it needs to go without you manually connecting the dots.

Before and After: The Worked Example

Before: Manual Process

Monday morning. 15 customer emails arrived overnight with feedback. You open email (2 min), copy the first feedback (1 min), open your Packaged AI tool (30 sec), paste feedback (30 sec), submit and wait (1 min), copy response (30 sec), open Gmail (30 sec), click reply (30 sec), paste response (30 sec), edit it (2 min), send (30 sec), open spreadsheet (1 min), log it (1 min). Then repeat for the next email.

Total: 4 hours for 15 responses. AI did 5 minutes of work. You did 3 hours 55 minutes of connector work.

After: Automated Workflow

The trigger watches your email inbox. New feedback arrives. The workflow fires automatically. Data pulls from the email. Your Packaged AI processes it (5 minutes of actual work for all 15 responses). The response lands directly in the customer's inbox. Everything logs automatically.

Total: 10 minutes for you to monitor and verify. Everything else happens while you work on something else.

Key Insight

The AI is fast. You are slow. The workflow fixes that. Stop being the middleware.

Want to map your bottleneck? Ask your AI Buddy: "I currently do this work manually: [describe your process]. Every time I do it, here's what I do step by step: [list the steps]. Where am I doing the connector work? Where is the AI tool sitting unused between my manual steps?"
Brief 2 Framework

The 5-Part Workflow Blueprint

There are five parts to every workflow that automates business processes. Every platform uses them. Make.com, Zapier, n8n, Power Automate. They all speak the same language.

1. TRIGGER
What Starts It

The trigger is the starting signal. Something happens that kicks off the workflow. Triggers can be time-based (every Monday at 9 AM), event-based (new email, form submission, spreadsheet row), or manual (you click a button).

2. DATA
The Fuel

Data is what the workflow operates on. Sometimes the trigger brings it (the email includes customer name, content, date). Sometimes you fetch it (pull customer record from CRM for context). Sometimes you transform it (clean messy data).

3. AI STEP
What AI Does With It

This is where your Packaged AI from Mission 2 lives, now inside a system instead of in a chat window. Data flows to it automatically. It processes and produces output.

4. ACTION
Where Output Goes

The action is where the output lands. Send an email. Create a task. Post a message. Update a spreadsheet. This step replaces you as the manual connector.

5. LOG
The System's Memory

AI doesn't remember. Your logs become the system's memory. Every time the workflow runs, log: timestamp, input that triggered it, AI output generated, action status. When something breaks, your log tells you where and what happened.

Complete Example: Customer Feedback Workflow

Jordan's 5-Part Workflow

Trigger: New email arrives in feedback inbox. Data: Extract sender email, name, subject, body, timestamp. Look up sender in CRM. AI Step: Use Packaged AI with system prompt: "You are a customer success specialist. Use customer history to personalize. Be warm, specific, acknowledge their concern, offer a solution." Action: Send AI-generated response as reply via Gmail. Log: Write timestamp, customer name, feedback received, AI response, email sent status to feedback log spreadsheet.

Key Insight

The platform is the tool. The pattern is the skill. Learn it once, build on any platform.

Want to design your workflow? Ask your AI Buddy: "I want to automate this work: [describe it]. My Packaged AI from Mission 2 is: [describe it]. Using the 5-Part Workflow Blueprint, help me design each part. Be specific about what happens at each step."
Brief 3 Build

Build Your Trigger and Data Layer

Now you execute. Before you start, you have three things: your Packaged AI tool from Mission 2 (working and tested), your workflow design from Brief 2 (your 5-part blueprint written out), and access to an automation platform (Make.com account created).

Step 1: Create Your Scenario and Set Your Trigger

Log into Make.com. Click "Create a new scenario." Name it something clear: "Customer Feedback Response" or "Lead Qualification Workflow" or whatever your workflow does. Don't use generic names.

Select your trigger app. For a customer feedback workflow, it's Gmail. For a lead qualification workflow, it might be a form submission app. For a content review workflow, it might be a Slack channel. Configure the trigger: which inbox to watch, which form to monitor, which channel to listen to.

Test the trigger. Add a test input. Does the trigger fire? You should see it in the workflow run history. If yes, move to step 2. If no, check: Is the account connected correctly? Is the trigger looking at the right inbox or form or channel? Fix it and test again.

Key Point

Don't move forward until the trigger is working. Everything else depends on it.

Step 2: Extract and Prepare Data

Add a second module to your workflow. This is the Data step. Depending on your trigger, you might use a Gmail module to extract email content, a spreadsheet module to get row data, a form module to extract submission fields, or a Slack module to extract message content.

Configure it to capture all the data your AI step needs. If you need data from outside the trigger (customer history from your CRM, context from a previous interaction), add another module to fetch it.

Test with real data. Does the extracted data look right? Are the fields mapped correctly? If you see messy data, note it - you might need to clean it with a formatter step.

Key Point

Pay attention to data quality. What looks small here becomes a big problem at the AI step. Garbage in, garbage out.

Brief 4 Build

Wire the AI Step and Action

You have your trigger and data layer working. Now you connect your Packaged AI into a live workflow. This is where you remove yourself from the loop.

Step 3: Connect Your Packaged AI

Add your AI step. This is where your Packaged AI tool from Mission 2 comes in. If your Packaged AI lives in ChatGPT or Claude with a system prompt, use the OpenAI or Anthropic API module. Map your data fields to the prompt input.

If your Packaged AI is a custom tool (built in another platform), use an HTTP module to call it. You'll need the API endpoint and any authentication keys.

Feed your extracted data into the AI step. Include all context the AI needs to do its job well. Test with real data. Does the AI output look right? Is it personalized? Is it the quality you expect from Mission 2?

If the output is generic or wrong, the problem is usually the data (garbage in, garbage out) or the AI prompt needs refinement.

Key Point

This is where you might refine your Mission 2 Packaged AI. Real workflow data sometimes teaches you things your testing didn't.

Step 4: Configure the Action

Add your action step. This is where the output lands. If the action is send an email, use a Gmail module mapped to the customer's email address with the AI output as the body. If it's create a task, use Asana or similar with AI output as the task description. If it's update a spreadsheet, use Google Sheets with AI output in a specific column. If it's post a message, use Slack with AI output as the message content.

Map your fields carefully. The AI output field should go to the right place. Customer name or ID should go to the right recipient or record.

Test the action. Does the output arrive where it should? Check your email. Check the sheet. Check the app. Does it look right? Is the formatting correct? Is anything missing?

Key Point

This is where you remove yourself from the loop. Before today, you did this manually. Now the system does.

Brief 5 Build

Log, Test, and Ship

You have your trigger, data, AI step, and action all working. Now add the logging that gives your system memory. Then test the complete workflow. Then ship it.

Step 5: Log Everything

Add your log step. This is usually a spreadsheet or database module where you write back the results. Create columns for: Timestamp (when the workflow ran), Input (what triggered it - customer name, feedback content, lead source, etc.), AI Output (what the AI generated), Action Status (sent, failed, pending, etc.), and any other metadata you want to track.

Configure the module to write back to your spreadsheet or database. Map each field correctly. Test the log. Run the workflow and check: does the spreadsheet row get populated? Does the timestamp capture correctly? Does the AI output get recorded?

Key Point

Logs are how you build system memory. Skip this step and you lose visibility. Don't skip it.

Full Workflow Test

Now run the entire workflow end to end with real data. Add a test input to your trigger. Go check the workflow run history. Did the trigger fire? Did the data extract correctly? Did the AI step complete and look right? Did the action complete? Did the log capture everything? If all steps work, you just built a real automated workflow. Celebrate.

If something breaks, click the error. Read it. Most problems are simple: Connection expired? Re-authorize the app. Field mapping wrong? Fix the field mapping. Missing data? Check the data step. Permissions issue? Grant the necessary permissions. Rate limit hit? Wait or adjust the API call.

Key Point

Don't panic. Don't start over. Diagnose and fix.

Testing for Happy Path and Edge Cases

The happy path is the ideal scenario. New feedback arrives. The workflow triggers. Data extracts cleanly. AI generates a great response. Email sends successfully. Everything logs correctly.

But real data is messy. Not all inputs are ideal. Some feedback is unclear. Some emails are incomplete. Some customer records are missing. Test with real messy inputs. Feedback with special characters. Incomplete customer records. Missing data. Unusual scenarios. For each one, does the workflow handle it or break? If it breaks, note the error and fix it.

You don't need perfect. You need working. A workflow that handles 90 percent of your inputs well is ship-ready. It saves you time on 90 percent of cases. For the remaining 10 percent, you manually handle and log that it failed. You can improve it later.

Key Point

The enemy of shipped is perfect.

Key Takeaways

  • You are not doing the work. You are designing the system. Before today, you were the middleware manually connecting pieces. Your job now is to design a system where the connections happen automatically.
  • 95 percent of companies fail at AI not because the tools don't work, but because they don't have workflows figured out. A better AI tool doesn't help if a human still has to manually move the output around. Workflows remove you from the loop.
  • The 5-Part Blueprint works on any platform. Learn the pattern, not the tool. Make.com, Zapier, n8n, Power Automate. Different buttons. Same five parts: Trigger, Data, AI Step, Action, Log. When you move to a new platform, you already know how to build.
  • A good trigger means you don't have to remember. Set it and forget it. Triggers are what make automation actually automatic. The best triggers are the ones that fire without you thinking about them.
  • Your logs become the system's memory. Skip the log and you're flying blind. AI doesn't remember. When your workflow runs 100 times, your log has to remember what happened all 100 times. Without logs, you have no visibility into what your system is actually doing.
  • Things will break. Click the error. Read it. Most fixes are obvious. Every workflow you build will break at least once. This is normal. The skill isn't building perfect workflows. The skill is diagnosing the errors you get and fixing them.
