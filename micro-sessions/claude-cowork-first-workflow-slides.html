Agentic AI Essentials
Your First Cowork Workflow
Problem first. AI second. ROI always.
Welcome. In the next 30 minutes you are going to build a real AI program. Not a practice prompt. Not a demo you watch and forget. A working system that runs on your actual files, in your actual job. But before we get into how to build it - let me show you what one looks like when it is finished.
Slide 2 —
The Tool Was Not the Problem. But This Tool Is Really Good.
95% of companies get no ROI from AI.
Not because the technology doesn't work.
Because they skipped the process and grabbed the tool.
Today you get both.
MIT Sloan studied companies and their AI investments. 95 percent reported no measurable return. Not low return. No return. The pattern is always the same - someone sees a new tool, gets excited, skips straight to using it, and wonders why nothing changes. The tool was never the problem. The process was. But here is the honest truth about where we are right now: the tools have gotten genuinely extraordinary. Claude Cowork sits on your desktop, reads your files, follows your instructions, and builds things with you in real time. It is not a chatbot. It is a system builder. You just have to give it the right foundation.
Slide 3 —
Let Me Show You What I Actually Built
An email arrives. It has a blog idea inside.
I pick the idea. I answer two questions.
The post is written, formatted, and ready to publish.
I did not write a single word.
This is my blog-to-production workflow. Every few days, Cowork sends me an email with a content idea - generated from my notes, my recent conversations, things happening in the industry. I open the email, I pick the idea I want, I go back into the chat, I answer two questions about angle and audience, and it runs. By the time I close my laptop the post is finished. This is not hypothetical. This is running right now on my actual work. I built it in Cowork using five steps. Those five steps are what you are doing today.
Slide 4 —
A Quick Tour of Cowork
Task - where you work with Claude on your files
Scheduled - workflows that run automatically
Ideas - content and prompts Claude surfaces for you
Customize - your project instructions and folder settings
Before we build, let me show you what you are working with. When you open Cowork you will see four areas. Task is where you have a live conversation with Claude - you give it a message, it reads your folder, it does the work. Scheduled is where you set workflows to run on a timer - daily, weekly, whenever you need. Ideas is where Claude proactively surfaces suggestions based on your files and instructions. And Customize is where you set the project instructions - the permanent operating procedure for that folder. That last one is the most important part of what we are building today. Everything else is the interface. The project instructions are the system.
Slide 5 —
Define - Use Claude to Design the Workflow
Do not start with the tool.
Start with Claude.
Ask it to map and visualize your workflow first.
Here is how the Define phase actually works in practice. You open Claude and you describe the task you want to automate. Not to a tool. Not to a form. To Claude, in plain language. Tell it what you do, when you do it, what goes in, what comes out. Then ask it to map the workflow visually - a diagram, a step-by-step flow, a trigger-to-output picture. Claude will ask you clarifying questions. It will surface steps you forgot to mention. It will show you where AI fits and where it does not. You will understand your own workflow better after ten minutes with Claude than after an hour staring at a blank page. Do that first. Always.
Slide 6 —
Discover - Connect the Folder and Ask Claude What It Needs
Open Cowork. Select your folder.
Then ask Claude: what does this workflow need to run?
Let it tell you how to organize what you have.
Now you connect your data. Open Cowork and click Select Folder - point it at wherever the files for this task actually live. Then do not assume Claude can work with what is there. Ask it directly: "Look at this folder. What information do you need to run this workflow? What is missing? How should this be organized?" Claude will tell you. It might say the file names are inconsistent. It might say it needs a reference document you have not created yet. It might say the folder is perfectly set up. Either way, you are not guessing. You are doing a real data discovery - which is the step that 95 percent of organizations skip entirely.
Slide 7 —
Design - Ask Claude to Write the Instructions
You do not write the workflow instructions.
Claude does.
Tell it what you want. Ask it to implement it.
This is the part that surprises people. You do not sit down and write a long prompt or a set of rules. You tell Claude what you want the workflow to do - using the map you built in the Define phase - and you ask it to write the project instructions for you. Say something like: "Based on the workflow we mapped, write the project instructions for Cowork so it can run this every time." Claude will write a clear, structured instruction set, paste it into the Project Instructions field, and explain what it does. You review it. You adjust anything that does not feel right. Then it is done. The AI built its own operating procedure. That is the point.
Slide 8 —
Determine - What Does Success Actually Cost?
Does it work is not the question.
What is it worth is the question.
Set your ROI metric before you run it.
Before you run the workflow for the first time, you need a number. Not a feeling. A number. How long does this task take you today? How many times per week? What is your time worth per hour? Multiply it out. That is your baseline. Now run the workflow. Measure the time it just took. The difference is your ROI - and you should be able to state it in one sentence. "This workflow saves me four hours a week, which is worth about 10,000 dollars a year at my billing rate." That sentence is what you bring to your manager. That sentence is what turns a personal tool into an organizational program. Determine is not just debugging. It is building your business case.
Slide 9 —
Deploy - Your System Is Running
The instruction is saved.
The folder is connected.
The next session: how to push this further with GitHub.
Your workflow is deployed. The project instructions live in the folder. Anyone with Cowork can open this folder and run the workflow without a briefing, without context, without you. That is a system. That is what separates an AI Officer from someone who is just good at prompting. Now - in a couple of days we have a follow-up session on how to take what you just built and push it into GitHub so it becomes something your whole team can version, share, and improve. You do not need to know how to code. We are going to make GitHub something normal people can actually use.
