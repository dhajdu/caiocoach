Brief 1

From Ideas to Visuals

Most people open an AI image tool, type "professional product photo of an energy drink," and get something that looks like a stock photo from 2019. The gap between a generic AI image and a professional visual is the same gap you closed in Mission 3. Structure wins.

The 5-Element Framework

5-Element
Direct any AI image with precision
S
Subject - What is in the image? Be specific, not generic.
S
Style - Photorealistic, editorial, flat illustration, cinematic? Ask AI for options.
E
Environment - Where is this? Studio, outdoor, minimalist desk, neon-lit bar?
L
Lighting & Mood - The single biggest lever for changing the feel of an image.
D
Details & Enhancements - Depth of field, grain, camera angle, text overlay space.

Worked Example: Bare Prompt vs. 5-Element

Bare Prompt

"Create an image of an energy drink." Generic can, generic background, generic lighting. You'd never put this on a landing page.

5-Element Prompt

Subject: BOLT can, matte black, electric blue accent, condensation. Style: photorealistic. Environment: dark studio, single spotlight. Lighting: dramatic, high contrast. Details: shallow depth of field, reflection below. Looks like a professional photo shoot.

Key Insight

AI doesn't know what good looks like unless you tell it. The 5-Element Framework is how you tell it. Five decisions, defined before you generate, produce results that look professional instead of generic. Same principle from every mission: your input drives the output.

Brief 2

The VISION Framework for Video

Video starts with a controlled image. You don't describe a video from scratch. You take an image you already generated and extend it into motion with purpose.

VISION: Six Elements

VISION
Turn a still frame into motion with purpose
V
Visual Anchor - Your starting image. Frame one. Must be controlled and on-brand.
I
Intention - What emotion or message must land? Product reveal? Lifestyle moment?
S
Sequence - The movement, step by step. Camera pulls back. Hand reaches. Define it.
I
Immersion - The sensory layer. Lighting shifts, sound cues, texture. What stops the scroll?
O
Orbit - Camera motion. Static, slow zoom, pan, orbit, first-person POV.
N
Narrative Arc - Beginning, shift, end. Even in 6 seconds, there's a story.
Key Insight

Video without structure is just moving noise. VISION gives you six decisions to make before you generate, so the output has purpose. Same discipline: define the elements, let AI write the structured prompt, review it, run it. The AI Officer builds repeatable visual systems, not one-off clips.

Brief 3

Tools of the Trade

You need to know what's under the hood. Not to become a designer. To make the right tool decision for your program.

Three Types of Visual AI Tools

LLMs with image generation. ChatGPT (DALL-E), Claude, Gemini (Imagen, Nano Banana). You're already using these. Good for most business visuals.

Embedded AI. Canva, Figma, Adobe - AI built into design tools. Good when you need AI inside an existing workflow.

Purpose-built tools. Midjourney for artistic images. Runway for video. Google Flow for video generation. Use these when the LLMs can't give you what you need.

Key Insight

99% of visual AI apps are just wrappers around the same models. Before your team signs up for another $20/month tool, ask what's under the hood. If it's DALL-E with a pretty interface, you can do the same thing for free in ChatGPT. The AI Officer knows the difference.

Brief 4

Consistency at Scale: Style DNA

One good image is easy. Eleven images and two videos that all look like they came from the same brand? That's a system.

What Is Style DNA?

A document that locks your brand's visual identity. It defines what stays fixed across every visual (colors, typography style, overall mood) and what varies by audience and platform (composition, environment, energy level).

Fixed vs. Variable

Fixed Elements

Color palette, typography style, logo treatment, overall mood, quality level. Same across every image and video.

Variable Elements

Composition, environment, energy level, camera angle, aspect ratio. Shift by audience and platform while staying on-brand.

Key Insight

The leadership test is not "can you make a good image?" It's "could Dana hand this entire visual system to a new associate on day one and get consistent, on-brand visuals without a single meeting?" If yes, you built a system. If not, you made some pictures.
