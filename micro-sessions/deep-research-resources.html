Tools & Links

The tools used in this session and the best sources for deep research on AI topics.

Tool 01 ↗
NotebookLM
notebooklm.google.com

The primary tool from this session. Free to use with a Google account. Upload academic papers, reports, or documents and use NotebookLM to generate summaries, mind maps, audio overviews, and cited insights. Start here.
Source 01 ↗
MIT Sloan Management Review — AI Research
mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/artificial-intelligence

One of the most credible sources of applied AI research for business leaders. Articles are based on original research and written for practitioners, not just academics. Use this as a source collection for your NotebookLM notebooks.
Source 02 ↗
Stanford HAI — Human-Centered AI
hai.stanford.edu/research

Stanford's AI research hub, including the annual AI Index Report — one of the most comprehensive datasets on AI progress, adoption, and impact. Free to download. Excellent source material for deep research on AI trends and workforce impacts.
Source 03 ↗
Google Scholar
scholar.google.com

The fastest way to find peer-reviewed academic papers on any topic. Search for your research question, filter by date, and look for papers with high citation counts — those are the sources others have found credible enough to build on. Download PDFs and upload directly to NotebookLM.
Source 04 ↗
McKinsey Global Institute — AI Reports
mckinsey.com/mgi/our-research/artificial-intelligence

McKinsey's research arm publishes free reports on AI's economic impact, workforce transformation, and adoption patterns across industries. Well-cited, business-oriented, and updated regularly. Particularly useful for AI Officers making the business case for AI investment.
