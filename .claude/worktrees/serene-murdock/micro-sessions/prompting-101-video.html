03 Concept

Why Video Now

Forrester Research found that one minute of video is worth 1.8 million words. That sounds extreme until you think about your last five days of work communication.

How much of it was a long document nobody read? How much was a status update buried in a thread? Video cuts through. It is the format people actually watch. And until recently, making it cost hundreds of dollars and days of back-and-forth with a production team.

Before

A 400-word update. Your manager skims the first paragraph. The key message is in paragraph three. Nobody reads paragraph three.

Now

A 30-second video. Your face, your message, your energy. The whole thing in the time it takes to read one paragraph. And they actually watch it.

Key Insight

The leaders who figure out AI video first will change how their teams communicate. Not just for marketing - for every message that matters. That shift starts with one framework.

04 Framework

The VISION Framework

Perfect AI video is not magic. It is structure. The VISION framework gives you six elements that turn a vague idea into a prompt a video model can actually follow.

V
Visual anchor Lock identity with a reference image. Character, set, brand cues - everything the model needs to stay consistent across every frame.
I
Intention What is this scene for? An announcement, a demo, a mood piece, a CTA? Define the purpose before you describe the motion.
S
Sequence (beats) List 3 to 5 micro-actions in order. Not "things happen" - specific beats. Beat one, beat two, beat three. This is your shot list.
I
Immersion Lighting, time of day, texture, ambient sound, pace of cuts. This is the sensory layer - what does it feel like to be in this scene?
O
Orbit (camera) How the camera moves. Pan, dolly-in, tilt, rack focus, slow zoom, static wide shot. Name the movement and you control the energy.
N
Narrative One sentence. What changes from the opening frame to the closing frame? That is your story arc.
Director's tip

Write the beats first. Generate. Then adjust one lever at a time - orbit, then lighting, then emotion. That is how directors iterate. One variable at a time until it is right.

05 Exercise

Exercise: Build Your Reference Image

Everyone does this right now. Five minutes.

Open any image tool - Google Gemini, ChatGPT, or whatever you used last week. Design your ultimate dream workspace. Use the 5-element framework: subject, style, setting, composition, and details. One image. Save it. That image is the first frame of your video.

Basic prompt

"A nice office with a window view."

5-element prompt

"A young professional at a modern standing desk (subject), photorealistic editorial style (style), floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking Boracay (setting), medium wide shot centered on the desk (composition), warm golden light, plants, laptop open (details)."

Why the reference image matters

The reference image is your visual anchor - the V in VISION. Without it, AI video models drift. Characters change. Settings shift. With it, your brand, your character, your scene stay consistent from the first frame to the last.

06 Exercise

Exercise: Frames to Video in Google Flow

You have your reference image. Now you make it move.

1

Open Google Flow

Go to lab.google.com/flow. Create a new project. Choose Frames to Video - not text to video. You will see two boxes appear.

2

Upload your reference image

Upload your workspace image in the first box. This is your first frame. It locks your scene for the entire clip.

3

Paste your VISION prompt

Use the prompt from the chat or write your own. Add one line: "Use the reference image in the first frame as the anchor." This keeps everything consistent.

4

Set to two variations, landscape

Choose 2 variations so you can compare results. Keep the aspect ratio landscape. Hit generate. It takes about 30 seconds.

5

Review and share

Download your clip. Drop it in the chat. Look at what others made. Notice what the reference image kept consistent - and what it did not.

What just happened

You just did what used to require a production team and a budget. The reference image kept your scene consistent. The beats gave it a story. The orbit gave it motion. One framework, one session. That is a real new skill.

09 Recap

Key Takeaways

  • The reference image is the most important ingredient Without a visual anchor, AI video drifts. Characters change. Scenes lose consistency. The reference image is what keeps your brand and identity locked across every frame.
  • Beats turn vague prompts into reliable sequences The S in VISION is where most people cut corners. List the actions step by step. Three specific beats produce a real story. One vague sentence produces noise.
  • Camera language gives you director-level control Pan, dolly-in, rack focus, slow zoom. Learn five camera terms and you gain control over the entire energy of a clip. This is the orbit element.
  • Iterate one variable at a time Generate first, then adjust one thing - orbit, then lighting, then emotion. That is how directors work. One lever at a time until the output is right.
  • Video is now a leadership communication tool When you can make professional-quality video in 10 minutes, your ideas travel faster than everyone around you. That is not a design skill. That is a leadership advantage.
