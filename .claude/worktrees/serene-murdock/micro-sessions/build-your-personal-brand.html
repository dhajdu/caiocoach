03 Concept

The Problem

Your brand already exists. Whether you have defined it or not, people already have an impression of you - how you communicate, how you influence decisions, what you are known for on your team.

Most people leave this entirely to chance. And when they finally decide to define it, they ask AI to write their bio and get something that sounds like everyone else's bio. Generic. Flat. That is not an AI problem. That is a data problem.

Without the process

Ask AI: "Write my personal brand statement." Get back something about a "results-driven professional with a passion for innovation." Could belong to anyone. Belongs to no one.

With the process

Collect specific answers to specific questions. Run one prompt. Get back a brand document that actually sounds like you - because it came from you.

Key Insight

AI is accurate when you give it accurate data. The most valuable dataset you own is a set of honest answers about who you are and what you do. This session teaches you how to collect it and use it.

04 Step 1

Customize Your Interview Questions

A generic question like "what are you passionate about?" is hard to answer well. A question tailored to your specific industry and role - that's easy. You already know the answer.

Start by letting AI customize the questions for you. Paste your LinkedIn profile or a short summary of your background and run this prompt.

Prompt 1 - Question Customization
Here is my professional background: [paste your LinkedIn profile or a 2-3 sentence summary of your role, industry, and experience]. Based on this, customize these 5 brand interview questions to make them specific to my work: 1. What are you most passionate about in your field? 2. What do your best clients or colleagues rely on you for? 3. What problem do you solve that others don't? 4. What values guide how you work? 5. What do you want to be known for in 5 years? Make each question concrete and specific to my background. Replace generic language with language that reflects my actual industry and role.
Why this works

When you scraped a LinkedIn profile and customized the questions, you didn't need to know anything about the person in advance. The AI did the research for you. That's what makes this scalable - you can run this process for yourself, for a colleague, or for a client, starting from almost nothing.

05 Step 2 - Live Exercise

Answer the Questions

Everyone does this now. Two minutes. Write fast. Don't edit.

Take your customized questions and answer them - right here, right now, in a document or directly in the chat window. Your answers don't need to be polished. They need to be honest.

Weak answer

"I'm passionate about helping people achieve their goals and making a positive impact."

Strong answer

"I'm passionate about helping first-generation business owners in Southeast Asia stop making financial decisions by instinct. Most of them are growing fast but flying blind."

Key Insight

The difference is specificity. The strong answer names a real audience, a real problem, and a real tension. That's what gives the AI something to work with. The more specific your answers, the more distinctive your brand.

If you're in the room together

Pair up and read your answers out loud. When you hear yourself say something, you immediately know whether it's true. That's your editing process. If it sounds hollow out loud, rewrite it before you feed it to the AI.

06 Step 3

Run the Brand Exploration Prompt

Take your questions and answers and paste them into the brand exploration prompt. The AI will synthesize them into six sections: Purpose, Mission, Core Values, Tone of Voice, Unique Differentiators, and Brand Voice Guidelines.

The whole thing takes about 30 seconds to generate. Then you edit. Most people spend 2 to 3 hours refining before they're happy to share it - but what used to take weeks now takes a weekend.

Prompt 2 - Brand Exploration Document
I am going to give you a set of brand interview questions and my answers. Your job is to synthesize this into a brand exploration document. Produce the following six sections based only on what I have told you. Do not add things I have not said. Do not use generic branding language. 1. Purpose - The core reason I do what I do. One or two sentences. 2. Mission - What I am building or working toward. One or two sentences. 3. Core Values - 4 to 6 values. Each one: a label and one sentence describing what it means in practice. 4. Tone of Voice - How I naturally communicate. What to do and what to avoid. 4 to 6 specific notes. 5. Unique Differentiators - What makes me specifically different from others who do similar work. 3 to 4 points. 6. Brand Voice Guidelines - A short guide I can use as a custom instruction in an AI tool. Write it in second person ("Write in...") so it reads as an instruction. Here are my questions and answers: [paste your Q&A here]

What comes out

1

Purpose + Mission

Why you do what you do and what you are building toward. 1 to 2 sentences each.

2

Core Values

4 to 6 values with a short description of what each one means in practice - not just a word.

3

Tone of Voice

How you naturally communicate. What to do. What to avoid. Specific enough to be useful.

4

Unique Differentiators

What makes you specifically different from others who do similar work. Based on your answers - not invented.

5

Brand Voice Guidelines

A custom instruction you can store in ChatGPT or Claude so every future output sounds more like you.

07 Leadership

The Leadership Layer

Your brand document is not just a bio. It is a dataset. Once you have it, here is what becomes possible.

1

Custom AI instructions

Store your Brand Voice Guidelines in ChatGPT or Claude. Every output you generate from that point forward starts sounding more like you.

2

Team alignment

If you lead a team, this same process works for building a shared team brand or communications style. Run the interview with your team and produce a brand guide everyone can use.

3

Content production

Your brand doc becomes the brief for every piece of content you produce - LinkedIn posts, bios, speaking introductions, website copy. One source of truth, consistent output.

4

Scale to a brand book

The exploration document is the input for a full brand book. Hand it to a designer, or use it to brief a visual tool like Flow for visual direction. The words have to come first.

Key Insight

An AI Officer does not just use AI for personal tasks. They build systems others can run. The brand interview process you just learned works for clients, colleagues, and teams - not just yourself. That is the difference between a user and a leader.

09 Takeaways

Key Takeaways

  • Your data is the variable AI produces generic output when you give it generic input. Honest, specific answers about who you are and what you do produce a brand that actually sounds like you.
  • Questions are the framework A good set of customized questions does the hard thinking for you. You don't need to be a branding expert. You need a good prompt and honest answers.
  • The brand doc is a working tool Your Brand Voice Guidelines section belongs in your AI tools as a custom instruction. Every output you generate from this point forward should be filtered through it.
  • This process scales The same three steps that build your personal brand work for clients, team members, and organizational brand. Learning to run this for others is how you create value as an AI Officer.
10 Try This Week

Try This Week

Take your Brand Voice Guidelines section and paste it as a custom instruction in ChatGPT or Claude.

In ChatGPT: Settings - Personalization - Custom Instructions. In Claude: start a new conversation and paste your guidelines at the top as a system-level instruction.

Then write something - a LinkedIn post, an email, a summary. Compare the output to what you would have gotten without the guidelines. That difference is your brand starting to work for you.

The leadership version

Once you have your own brand doc, offer to run the same process for a colleague or a report. Take their LinkedIn profile, customize the questions, record a 15-minute conversation, and produce their brand exploration doc as a gift. That is AI leadership in action - not just using the tool for yourself.
