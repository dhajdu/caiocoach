Mission 1 — Challenge Prompts

Every prompt is copy-paste ready. Replace anything in [brackets] with your specific information. Use these with any AI tool — ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.

💡 How to use these: Copy any prompt, paste it into your AI tool, fill in the brackets with your real context. The more specific you are in the brackets, the better the output. Generic answers get generic results.
Brief 1 — The Other 50%
Starter Prompt
Map Your AI Half
Understand what the AI Officer role looks like specifically in your job
I'm a [your job title] at a [your company type]. I want to understand what the AI half of my leadership role looks like. Ask me about my daily work, my team, and where I spend the most time. Then tell me which parts of my job are 'leading people' and which parts could become 'leading AI' — meaning where I could design an AI program to deliver better results.
Brief 2 — The AI Officer Mindset
Starter Prompt
Practice the 5 Daily Moves
Walk through all five daily moves on a real task from your work
I want to practice the five daily moves of an AI Officer on a real task from my work. Ask me about my role and what I spend the most time on. Then help me pick one task and walk through all five moves: find the problem worth solving, identify the data, decide build or buy, design how to orchestrate it, and sketch a plan.
Brief 3 — What is AI?
Starter Prompt
AI Can vs. Can't — Your Industry
Get specific AI capability examples tailored to your role and industry
I want to understand the difference between what AI can and can't do. Explain it to me using examples from my industry. Ask me what industry I'm in and what kind of work I do, then give me three things AI would be great at in my role and three things where I'd still need human judgment.
Brief 4 — The Path to 99% Accuracy
Starter Prompt
Bare Prompt → RACE → AI Interview
Practice the full progression and see the difference in output quality
I want to practice the progression from bare prompt to RACE to AI Interview. Give me a simple business task — like writing an email or summarizing a report — and walk me through all three approaches so I can see the difference in the output.
Challenge 1 — C1
See How AI Follows Your Lead
Ask the same question four different ways. See how your input changes everything.
I'm going to ask you the same question four different ways. After each version, tell me what you're able to infer about the task and what gaps you have. Show me how the output quality changes.

Question 1 (bare): "Write an email about a new product."

Question 2 (with role): "You are a marketing manager. Write an email about a new product."

Question 3 (with RACE): "Role: Marketing manager at a beverage company. Action: Write a launch email to retail partners. Context: New energy drink called BOLT, targeting health-conscious millennials, $3.99, clean ingredients. Example: Professional but energetic tone."

Question 4 (AI Interview): Before writing anything, ask me 3-4 questions that will help you write a much better email than the previous versions.
Challenge 2 — C2
From Bare Prompt to AI Interview
Write the same email three ways. See the jump from generic to professional.
I need to write an email for [describe your task] at my company.

First, write it from just this information. Don't ask me anything.

Then, write it again using the RACE framework. I'll fill in:
- Role: [who the AI is acting as]
- Action: [what it should write]
- Context: [the situation, audience, key details]
- Example: [what good looks like for tone/format]

Finally, before writing a third version, ask me 3-4 questions. Then write the best version you can.

Compare all three. Tell me what changed and why.
Challenge 3 — C3
Prove It — The BOLT Newsletter
No template. No rounds. Deliver a newsletter announcement worth sending.
You are a senior copywriter at Buddy Bevs, a premium beverage company. Dana Reyes, our VP of Marketing, needs a company newsletter announcement for our new product BOLT launching next month.

BOLT is a clean-ingredient energy drink targeting health-conscious millennials. Key facts:
- Price: $3.99 per can
- Positioning: Clean energy — no artificial flavors, no artificial colors
- Pilot data: 87% repurchase intent in test markets
- Available in three flavors: Citrus Surge, Berry Blast, and Original

The announcement goes to our full company — about 200 people. Dana wants people to feel proud and excited. Tone: energetic, professional, not overly corporate.

Write a newsletter-ready announcement. One draft. Make it something Dana would approve without editing.
Brief 5 — Meet the Models
Starter Prompt
Choose Your AI Kingdom
Get a personalized recommendation on which AI tool to commit to
I'm trying to decide which AI tool to commit to for my certification program. Ask me about my work environment, the tools my team uses, and what kind of tasks I want to start with. Then recommend which AI Kingdom is the best fit and explain why.
Final Project — Your AI Learning Plan
Step 1
Self-Assessment
Honest self-assessment of where you are with AI today
I'm a [your job title] at a [describe your organization]. I want to do an honest self-assessment of my current AI skills and readiness.

Ask me questions one at a time about:
1. How I currently use AI (if at all)
2. What my biggest challenges at work are right now
3. What I wish AI could do for me
4. What's holding me back from using AI more
5. What my goals for the next 90 days are

After you've asked all questions, give me a self-assessment summary with a score from 1-10 on AI readiness and an honest assessment of my gaps.
Step 2
Build the Learning Plan
Turn your self-assessment into a personalized 8-week plan
Here is my AI self-assessment:
[paste your self-assessment summary]

Build me a personalized 8-week AI learning plan based on this. The plan should:
- Fit within [X hours per week] of available time
- Focus on what matters most for my specific role
- Include one concrete practice task per week (not just reading)
- Build toward the goal of: [your 90-day goal]

Format it as a week-by-week plan with a clear focus for each week and the one task I should complete.
Step 3
Quality Check Before Submitting
Run this before submitting your final project
Here is my AI Learning Plan:
[paste your plan]

Review this as a tough but fair coach. Tell me:
1. Is this specific to my role, or does it sound generic?
2. Are the weekly tasks actually doable in the time I said, or are they unrealistic?
3. What is missing that someone at my level should have in their plan?
4. If Dana Reyes asked to see this plan right now, would I be confident showing it?

Give me specific edits, not general feedback.
