03 Context

The Old Way Is Gone

Think about how shopping worked even two years ago. Open a search engine. Type a keyword. Get a list of links. Click through several pages. Open three tabs to compare prices. Read reviews. Decide. Checkout.

Every single step required you to be present and clicking. AI was invisible - hidden in recommendation algorithms - but it never talked to you and it never acted for you.

Shopping before AI

Search. Click. Browse. Compare. Read reviews. Click again. Fill in a form. Maybe buy. Return if it is wrong. Repeat for every purchase.

Shopping with AI

Tell the AI what you need. It finds options, compares them, explains the trade-offs, and - increasingly - completes the purchase for you. One conversation. Done.

Key Insight

The shift is not just in how people find products. It is in who does the work. AI is moving from advisor to agent - from helping you shop to shopping for you.

04 Concept

Agentic Commerce

The term you will hear everywhere in 2026 is agentic commerce. Here is what it means.

In a traditional search, the AI helps you find something and then hands control back to you. You click the link. You fill in the form. You enter your card details. You confirm. In agentic commerce, the AI completes the transaction on your behalf. You give it intent and budget - it does everything else.

"I need flowers delivered to my girlfriend by 6 PM tonight. Under 500,000 dong. Something romantic."
The AI finds the options, picks the best match, processes the payment, confirms the delivery time. You approved it. The AI did it.

This is not science fiction. ChatGPT has a wallet. Perplexity has buy links. Amazon's Rufus assistant answers product questions and guides checkout. The infrastructure is here. The scale is building.

Key Insight

The transition from "AI as search" to "AI as buyer" is the biggest shift in e-commerce since the credit card went online. Businesses that understand it now will be positioned for the wave. Businesses that wait will be chasing it.

05 Landscape

The Platform Landscape

Here is where each major platform stands right now. This is a fast-moving space - check for updates as each platform expands.

ChatGPT
Live + Expanding
Product cards with images, pricing, and reviews. Checkout live for Shopify and Stripe merchants. Any business with a Shopify store can connect their catalog. Expected to expand to more platforms rapidly.
Perplexity
Live
Strong real-time price comparison and product research. Buy links and product cards in search results. Best for research-heavy or specification-driven purchases where customers compare before buying.
Amazon Rufus
Growing
Conversational AI built into the Amazon app. Answers detailed product questions and navigates the catalog. Amazon moves slower on this but has the largest product catalog and most purchase intent data in the world.
Google Gemini
Growing
Deep integration with Google Shopping, real-time pricing, store locations, reviews, and YouTube content. G Pay checkout potential. Google's challenge: protecting search ad revenue while enabling AI shopping.
The China wildcard

Alibaba has not made big moves on AI shopping yet - but they have the infrastructure, the catalog, and the customer base to move fast when they decide to. For businesses selling into Southeast Asia and China, watching the Alibaba AI commerce roadmap is worth your attention. It is quiet now. It will not stay quiet.

07 Context

The Trust Question - And Why It Does Not Matter

The first objection to AI shopping is always trust. Giving an AI access to your payment information to buy things on your behalf sounds alarming. It is also a pattern we have seen before.

"Nobody will ever put their credit card number on a website."
Every normal person, 1997. Then Amazon made it too convenient to refuse.

The pattern repeats with every new commerce format. Nobody would share their location with a food app - until Grab made delivery too convenient to avoid. Nobody would store their card in a phone - until Apple Pay removed every friction point. The trust objection is always real. The convenience always wins.

The trust objection

Giving an AI my card details feels risky. What if it buys something wrong? What if there is fraud? I want to control every purchase.

The convenience reality

You approved it in a voice command. It arrived on time. You saved 20 minutes. The flowers were perfect. You do it again the next day.

Key Insight

For AI Officers, the trust question is not personal - it is strategic. When will your customers cross the trust threshold? That timing determines when you need to be ready. In the US: likely 2026. In Southeast Asia: likely 2027-2028. Prepare before the crossing, not after.

08 Strategy

What This Means for Your Business

If you sell anything - products, services, courses, food - here is what you need to be thinking about now.

1

Your product catalog is now your storefront

AI shopping pulls from product feeds, schema markup, and catalog APIs. Vague descriptions, missing specifications, and low-quality images make you invisible. Clean, structured, machine-readable data is the foundation. Fix this first.

2

GEO is the new SEO

Generative Engine Optimization - getting found inside AI platforms - requires different tactics than Google ranking. Detailed specifications, authentic reviews, FAQ content, and structured data are what AI shopping engines index. The playbook is being written right now.

3

The early mover window is open

AI shopping advertising does not exist yet at scale. Organic visibility is cheap right now. Early Google Ads buyers in 2002 paid pennies per click. The same window is open today in AI shopping. It will not stay open. This is the gold rush moment.

4

LLM presence is a new channel to manage

Just as you manage your Google presence, your social media, and your app store listings - you will soon manage your LLM presence. Tools for this are being built. Knowing this is coming lets you build the data foundation before the tools require it.

The Shopify move

If you sell physical products and you are not on Shopify yet, that is worth reconsidering. Shopify is the first non-Etsy platform to connect to ChatGPT checkout, and it is the fastest path to becoming shoppable inside AI conversations. A basic Shopify store with a clean product catalog can be set up in a day. The integration with ChatGPT is the compelling reason to do it now rather than later.

10 Recap

Key Takeaways

  • Agentic commerce is here. It is rolling out now. ChatGPT has checkout for Shopify merchants. Perplexity has buy links. Amazon is building. Google is integrating. This is not a future trend - it is a present reality expanding fast.
  • The shift is from AI finding to AI buying. In the old model, AI helped you search. In the new model, AI completes the transaction. The agent does not just advise - it acts on your behalf with the budget and intent you give it.
  • Trust will not stop this. Convenience always wins. Every new commerce format faces a trust barrier. It always falls once convenience crosses the threshold. The pattern from online credit cards to mobile payments to AI wallets is the same.
  • Your product data is now your competitive advantage. AI shopping runs on clean, structured product information. Detailed descriptions, clear pricing, real specifications, and authentic reviews are what get you found. GEO - Generative Engine Optimization - is the new SEO.
  • The early mover window is open. Not for long. AI shopping advertising does not exist at scale yet. Organic visibility is cheap. The businesses that get their catalogs connected and their data clean now will own the early rankings - just like early Google Ads buyers owned search in 2002.
