Brief 1: The Setup Time Problem

The Hidden Cost

Here's what nobody measures: the time you spend setting up AI before you can actually use it. You open a tool. You explain your role. You upload the same file you uploaded yesterday. You describe the format you need. This takes 5 minutes.

That doesn't sound like much. But it happens 4 times a day, 5 days a week.

Setup per use
5 min
Per week
100 min
Per year
86 hrs
Per team (10 people)
860 hrs

Nobody tracks it because it feels like working. Setup time is invisible until you measure it. Then it's everywhere.

Good Prompts Don't Scale

You spend 20 minutes crafting a perfect prompt. You get exactly what you need. And it disappears into chat history. Next week you need the same thing and you start from scratch. The prompt worked. The system didn't.

Key Insight

Setup time is the invisible tax on every AI user. Packaging is how you eliminate it.

The Worked Example

Sarah's situation: She's a marketing manager. Every day, teams ask her to turn rough ideas into brief formats. She opens Claude. She pastes the brand guide. She explains the template. She shows an example. She uploads the request. Five minutes. Four times a day.

Without Packaging

  • Open AI tool
  • Paste brand guide
  • Explain template
  • Show example
  • Paste request
  • Get output

5 minutes x 4 times = 20 minutes daily

With Packaging

  • Open Content Brief Generator
  • Pick a conversation starter
  • Type your idea
  • Get output

2 minutes x 4 times = 8 minutes daily

Component 1: Data

This is what your AI knows. Not what the user tells it. What it already has built in.

  • Reference documents and guidelines
  • Templates and examples
  • FAQs and policies
  • Style guides and tone samples

Rule: Upload real files. Not summaries. Not descriptions. The actual documents your team uses.

Component 2: Instructions (RACE)

This is what your AI does. Specifically. Using RACE:

  • Role: Who is the AI acting as?
  • Action: What exactly does it do?
  • Context: What constraints? What format? What always? What never?
  • Example: What does good output look like?

Component 3: Interface

This is how users interact:

  • Name: "Email Tone Adjuster" (tells someone what it does)
  • Description: One sentence. What? For whom? Format?
  • Conversation Starters: Three pre-written prompts for common uses
Key Insight

Get all three components right and your team can use it without training. Miss one and you become the manual.

Brief 3: Platform Options

The Same Pattern, Three Platforms

All three major platforms support Packaged AI. The names are different. The architecture is identical.

ChatGPT Custom GPTs

How it works: Upload files, write system instructions, add name and description. Users access via ChatGPT.

Data: File uploads

Instructions: System prompt

Interface: Name, description, conversation starters

Claude Projects

How it works: Upload files, write custom instructions, name your project. Separate context window per project.

Data: Project files

Instructions: Custom instructions

Interface: Project name, description, welcome message

Gemini Gems

How it works: Upload context, write custom instructions, name your Gem. Access via Gemini.

Data: Custom context

Instructions: Custom instructions

Interface: Name, description, conversation starters

Key Insight

The platform is the tool. The pattern is the skill. Learn it once, build on any platform.

Brief 4: Build Your Prototype

Five Steps to Your First Packaged AI

Step 1: Create and Name Your Tool (2 min)

Open your platform. Create a new Custom GPT, Project, or Gem. Give it a name that explains what it does. Write a one-sentence description.

Step 2: Write Your System Prompt (5 min)

Use RACE format:

RACE Template 
Role: [Who is this AI acting as? Be specific.]

Action: [What exactly does it do? Real actions, with specifics.]

Context: [What are the rules? What format? What always? What never?]

Example: [Show good output. Show bad output and why.]

Step 3: Identify and Upload Your Data (5 min)

What does your AI need to know? Upload the actual files your team uses:

  • Reference documents and style guides
  • Templates and examples
  • FAQs and policies
  • Decision frameworks

Step 4: Add Conversation Starters (3 min)

Think about three common uses. Write conversation starters that guide users:

  • Starter 1: [First common use]
  • Starter 2: [Second common use]
  • Starter 3: [Third common use]

Step 5: Test the Happy Path (5 min)

Use a real example from your work. Does it work the way you designed it? Is the output usable?

Key Insight

The leadership test isn't whether it works for you. It's whether someone else on your team can open it on day one and get usable output without asking you anything.

Your AI Buddy Can Help

"I'm building a Packaged AI for [my item]. Walk me through exactly how I would write my RACE system prompt with role, action, context, and example."

Brief 5: Test and Ship

Validation Before Handoff

You've built your Packaged AI. You tested the happy path. Now validate it works for others.

Practice Challenges

Challenge 1

Map Your Data Layer (10 min)

What data does your Packaged AI need to know? List all reference documents, guidelines, templates, examples.

What good looks like: A specific list of 5-10 real documents.

Your AI Buddy Can Help

"What data should I upload for my Packaged AI on [my item]? List specific documents and files."

Challenge 2

Write Your RACE System Prompt (15 min)

Write your complete RACE system prompt for your Packaged AI. 200-400 words, specific to your domain.

What good looks like: Every section (Role, Action, Context, Example) filled with real specifics.

Your AI Buddy Can Help

"Help me write my RACE system prompt for [my item]. Here's my problem statement: [paste]."

Challenge 3

Design Your Interface (10 min)

Write your tool's name, one-sentence description, and three conversation starters.

What good looks like: A clear name, focused description, three distinct conversation starters for common uses.

Your AI Buddy Can Help

"Design my interface. Give me a name for my Packaged AI on [my item], a description, and three conversation starters."

Challenge 4

Test Your Build (10 min)

Create your Packaged AI. Test it with: happy path, edge case 1, edge case 2. Document results.

What good looks like: Three test runs with clear documentation of what worked and what didn't.

Your AI Buddy Can Help

"I'm testing my Packaged AI. Give me three test cases: one happy path, two edge cases that might break it."

Final Project: Your Packaged AI Prototype

This is your deliverable. Submit four parts:

Key Takeaways

Setup time is the hidden cost

Five minutes of setup times four daily uses times five days equals 86 hours per year. Package your work and eliminate it.

Three components, all required

Data (what your AI knows), Instructions (what your AI does via RACE), Interface (how users interact). Miss one and your team needs training.

The leadership test is the filter

Could a new hire use this on day one without asking you anything? If no, keep iterating on your data, instructions, or interface.

Good prompts don't scale

You can write a perfect prompt. It disappears into chat history. Package it once. Use it forever.

Semi-automated is the bridge

Manual is typing everything every time. Automated is agents running alone. Semi-automated is you triggering, AI executing with your standards built in.

Platform doesn't matter. Pattern does

ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini all support the same pattern. Learn it once, build on any platform.

Checkpoint: Knowledge Check

Question 1

If a team member spends 5 minutes setting up an AI tool 4 times daily, 5 days per week, how many hours per year is that on setup alone?

Correct Answer: B - 5 min × 4 times × 5 days = 100 min/week. 100 min/week × 52 weeks = 5,200 min/year. 5,200 min ÷ 60 = 86.67 hours/year.

Question 2

What are the three components of Packaged AI?

Correct Answer: B - Every Packaged AI tool has Data (what it knows), Instructions (what it does, using RACE), and Interface (how users interact).

Question 3

RACE stands for:

Correct Answer: B - RACE is a framework for writing clear system prompts: Role (who is the AI), Action (what it does), Context (constraints and rules), Example (what good looks like).

Question 4

The leadership test for Packaged AI is:

Correct Answer: C - The leadership test is whether someone else on your team could use your tool successfully on their first day without asking you for guidance.
