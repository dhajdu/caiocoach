Lesson 3

Learning Guide

Alright Cadets, before you jump in, here's how to make this mission actually work for you.

Know when to use a structured prompt. You don't need a perfect prompt every time. A quick casual question gets you what you need most of the time. But the moment your output is going to your manager or a leadership meeting, that's when structured prompting matters. Today you learn how to build structured prompts. You'll always know when to use them.

Do every challenge. They're not practice exercises. Each one produces a real deliverable that goes directly into your BOLT go-to-market brief. Skip one and Brief 5 has a hole in it.

Use the AI Interview before writing any prompt. It is the highest-leverage thing you will learn today. Tell AI what you want, ask it to ask you questions first, and answer honestly. The more honest your answers, the better the output.

Run VRC before you send anything. Creating output is only half the job. The other half is deciding whether it's ready to send. Every AI Officer runs VRC before putting anything in front of their manager.

How to Get the Most Out of This Session

  • Stack your frameworks: RACE first, then CRA on top. Two passes, not two separate processes.
  • Never skip the AI Interview: It finds the gaps you didn't know were there.
  • Let AI teach you: Every session is a chance to learn a framework you didn't know before.
  • Always run VRC: Send it, fix it, or start over. Decide before anyone else sees it.
  • Save your work: Everything you build in Briefs 1-4 is raw material for Brief 5.
Pro Tip

Start a new project or thread for this session. Upload your cleaned BOLT dataset from Mission 2 at the top. That way every challenge has clean context and you're not copying and pasting between tabs.

The Perfect Prompt: 8-Step Checklist

Use all eight steps when your output has your name on it. For everyday work, a quick prompt is fine. But when it matters, this is the sequence:

  1. Start a new thread or project. Clean context every time.
  2. Upload your data. Real inputs, not placeholders.
  3. Tell your AI Buddy what you want to accomplish. Goal first.
  4. Ask your AI Buddy to ask you questions. This is the AI Interview.
  5. Get a recommended framework. Let AI suggest the structure.
  6. Ask your AI Buddy to write the prompt. Then review it.
  7. Check it yourself. Lock numbers, audit math, surface assumptions.
  8. Run it. Then evaluate with VRC before sending.

Rules of the Road: Using Data Correctly

RuleWhat to say
Lock the numbers"Do not make up numbers." AI fills in the blanks confidently. Don't let it.
Audit the math"Check all computations." AI makes errors and presents them like facts.
Surface assumptions"List every assumption you made." AI makes decisions you didn't authorize.
Cite the source"Only use information from the attached file." Prevents hallucinations.
Ask for gaps"If something is missing, ask before assuming." Don't let AI fill blanks with guesses.

Brief 1

Stack Your Frameworks: RACE + CRA

You already have RACE from Mission 1. Today you add CRA, and learn how stacking them transforms a decent result into something you'd actually send.

Frameworks Overview

RACE
Defines the task

Use whenever you want AI to perform something specific.

R
Role — Who is the AI acting as?
A
Action — What should it do?
C
Context — What's the situation?
E
Example — What does "good" look like?
CRA
Locks the quality

Use as a quality control layer on top of your RACE draft.

C
Constraints — What are the limits?
R
Requirements — What must be included?
A
Acceptance Criteria — How do you know it's good enough?
Key Insight

RACE alone gets you 70% there. RACE + CRA gets you something your boss can use. Think of them as two passes, not two separate processes. First pass gets the content. Second pass tightens it to the standard you need. If you can't define what "good" looks like before you start, you'll never know if AI got there.

Brief 2

The AI Interview: The Master Skill

This is the most important thing you will learn today. Not the most important thing in this brief. The most important thing in the entire session.

What Is the AI Interview?

Instead of trying to write the perfect prompt, you tell AI what you want to accomplish and say: "Before you start, ask me every question you need answered."

AI asks you questions. You answer them. AI uses your answers to build something specific to your situation. Not generic. Yours.

Why This Changes Everything

Without It

You guess what AI needs. Output is generic. It could have come from anyone.

With It

AI finds the gaps you didn't know you had. Output sounds like it came from someone who actually knows your business.

The AI Interview flips the whole process. Instead of you anticipating everything AI needs to know, AI asks you the questions you didn't think to answer: who is the audience, what tone, what to avoid, what has already been tried.

Key Insight

The gap between what you would have written and what AI asks. That is exactly what makes this the master skill. AI finds what you didn't know was missing.

Brief 3

Let AI Teach You Frameworks On Demand

You don't need to memorize frameworks. You need to know how to ask for the right one.

There are hundreds of business frameworks. You will never memorize them all and you don't need to. What you need is the habit of asking AI to teach you the right one for the problem in front of you, and then applying it immediately.

The AI Officer Doesn't Memorize. The AI Officer Asks.

  1. Ask AI: "What framework should I use for this problem?"
  2. Ask AI: "Explain it simply, then apply it to my situation."
  3. You learn the framework and get the deliverable at the same time.
Key Insight

Every time you use AI, you have the opportunity to learn something. The AI Officer who asks "what framework should I use for this?" and then applies it immediately is learning faster than anyone in a traditional training program. This skill compounds. Every session you leave knowing something new.

Brief 4

VRC: Check Before You Send

You've learned to create. Now you learn to evaluate. This is the section that separates people who generate from people who lead.

VRC: Value, Risk, Checkpoint

Run VRC before you put your name on anything. Especially before sending to your manager, a client, or a leadership meeting.

VRC
Checks it before you send
V
Value — Where does the benefit come from? Is the payoff clear?
R
Risk — What are the top two ways this fails?
C
Checkpoint — What's the early signal it's working, before you're too far in?
Key Insight

RACE and CRA help you create. VRC helps you decide. Should you trust this output? What could go wrong? Every AI Officer runs VRC before putting anything in front of their manager. The question is always: send it, fix it, or start over.
