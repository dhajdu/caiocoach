03 Reframe

The Question Most People Never Ask

Most platform decisions start with the wrong question. Which AI is smartest? Which one has the best reviews? Which one does my competitor use?

None of these answers tell you which platform is right for your organization.

The wrong question

"Which AI is the most capable overall?" Leads to picking the platform with the best benchmark scores or the biggest marketing budget.

The right question

"What do we need agents to actually DO, and in what context?" Leads to picking the platform that fits your specific use cases, team, and infrastructure.

Key Insight

Get the platform choice wrong and you will spend two years migrating. The question is not which AI is smartest. The question is which ecosystem fits the work your team actually needs to automate.

04 Platform

ChatGPT: The Broadest Ecosystem

ChatGPT is the platform with the largest footprint. Millions of users, the biggest integration ecosystem, and the lowest barrier to entry for non-technical teams.

Strongest at
Breadth and accessibility

Largest library of pre-built GPTs. Best for non-technical teams who need to build and deploy agents quickly without engineering resources.

Best use cases
Marketing, commerce, content

Agentic commerce integration with Shopify. Strong for content workflows, customer-facing tools, and teams that live in a browser.

Watch out for
Depth of customization

Less control over agent behavior at the enterprise level. Better for horizontal tools than deep vertical integrations into specific business processes.

Geographic availability
Worldwide

Available globally with consistent performance across most regions including Southeast Asia.

05 Platform

Claude: The Extensibility Play

Claude is the platform for teams that need to build deeply customized agent systems. The differentiator is the Model Context Protocol - an open standard that lets Claude connect to virtually any data source or tool.

Strongest at
Custom integrations and depth

MCP enables connections to any tool or data source. Claude Code, Cowork, and Dispatch create a full agentic stack for document-heavy and file-heavy organizations.

Best use cases
Document processing, technical workflows

Best for organizations that need agents deeply integrated into their specific business systems and workflows. Strong for engineering-adjacent teams.

Watch out for
Setup complexity

The depth comes with setup requirements. Smaller teams without technical resources may find the initial configuration harder than ChatGPT.

Geographic availability
Worldwide

Available globally. MCP-based integrations work regardless of geography.

06 Platform

Gemini: The Google Org Play

Gemini is the right choice for organizations that run on Google Workspace. Its power is in the depth of integration with tools your team already uses every day.

Strongest at
Google Workspace automation

Native integration with Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Drive, and Calendar. If your team lives in Google tools, Gemini agents can operate inside those tools directly.

Best use cases
Google-native organizations

Jules coding agent is exceptionally strong. Google Search grounding gives real-time information access. Strong for organizations doing heavy Sheets-based analysis.

Watch out for
Outside the Google ecosystem

Gemini's power drops significantly if your team uses Slack instead of Google Chat, or Salesforce instead of Google's tools. Its strength is its specificity.

Geographic availability
Some regional variation

Certain Gemini features have regional availability limitations. Verify availability in your primary operating geography before committing.

07 Framework

The Selection Framework

Score each platform on five dimensions for your top three use cases. Use a 1-3 scale: 1 = poor fit, 2 = acceptable, 3 = strong fit.

1

Depth of autonomous action

Can this platform do the work with minimal human handoff for your specific use case?

2

Integration ecosystem fit

Does this platform connect to the tools your team already uses?

3

Team and admin controls

Does this platform give your IT and leadership team the visibility and control they need?

4

Geographic availability

Is this platform fully available where your team works?

5

Price per outcome

Does the pricing model match how you would use this platform at the scale you need?

Key Insight

Your recommendation is not the platform that wins all five. It is the platform that wins the dimensions that matter most for your three use cases. That distinction is the difference between a product preference and a leadership decision.
