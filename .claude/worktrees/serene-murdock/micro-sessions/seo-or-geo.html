03 Concept

The Shift

Think about how you searched for something last week. Did you open a list of ten links and click through them? Or did you type a question into an AI tool and read the answer it gave back?

Most people are already using AI to search - even if they do not call it that. Google Gemini, ChatGPT, and Perplexity now answer questions directly. They summarize the best available content and present the answer. No click required.

Old search

Someone types a question. They see a list of links. They choose one to click. Your goal: appear as high on that list as possible.

New search

Someone types a question. An AI reads thousands of sources, picks the most trustworthy, and gives a direct answer. Your goal: be the source it quotes.

Key Insight

SEO gets you found. GEO gets you cited. The game has not ended - the winning condition has changed.

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization. It is the practice of writing content that AI models can trust, understand, and quote. The rules are learnable. The advantage goes to whoever builds the habit first.

04 Framework

The E-E-A-T Framework

AI models do not just look for relevant content. They look for trustworthy content. The framework they use is called E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authority, and Trust.

These are not abstract ideals. They are signals you can build into every piece of content you write.

E

Experience

Share firsthand, real-world knowledge. Not "here is what experts say" - "here is what I saw happen when I did this." Specific stories beat general advice every time.

E

Expertise

Prove your knowledge with specifics. Link to your own case studies, projects, or results. Numbers beat adjectives. "We helped a team cut reporting time from 3 hours to 20 minutes" beats "we improved efficiency significantly."

A

Authority

Be referenced by others. Public speaking, published research, media mentions, and citations from other credible sources all build authority over time. Put your speaking calendar on your website even if it is not full yet.

T

Trust

Be accurate, consistent, and clearly attributed. A bio on the page. Credible citations linked to real research. A consistent voice and brand across all content. AI models verify that you are a real, accountable person.

Writing for AI

You are writing for models that summarize, not just people who scroll. Ask yourself after every paragraph: is there anything here an AI can verify? If not, make it specific enough that there is.

05 Tactics

GEO Tactics

The E-E-A-T framework tells you what to signal. These tactics tell you how to structure the writing so AI can actually use it.

Write in answerable units

AI models extract content in short, coherent chunks. Write in focused sections of 80-120 words, each one making a single point or answering a single question. If your writing takes a full page to get to the point, the model cannot easily pull a clean quote from it.

The test

Read each paragraph out loud as if an AI were reading it to someone who asked a question. Does it make sense on its own, without the rest of the article? If yes, it is quotable. If not, it needs to be tighter.

Cite smartly

AI models weigh content higher when it connects to a trusted web of information. Link to credible sources: universities, published research, verified public data. Not just any link - specific, traceable claims backed by real institutions.

Refresh existing content

You do not need to start from zero. Your best existing pages are the fastest wins. Find your top pieces, run them through the GEO rewrite prompt, and update them. The habit builds as you go.

Exercise: The Live Rewrite

GEO Rewrite Prompt
Rewrite this content for GEO (Generative Engine Optimization). Apply the E-E-A-T framework: add specific experience, demonstrate expertise with concrete examples, and make the writing trustworthy and citable by AI models. Keep the rewritten version to 80-120 words. Make each paragraph a clear, standalone answer that an AI could quote directly. Here is the original content: [PASTE YOUR CONTENT]

When you have your before-and-after, post both in the chat. Look at what changed. Most rewrites turn vague claims into specific, verifiable ones. That is the shift.

Before GEO

"I am a marketing consultant with experience helping companies improve their digital presence. I work with businesses of all sizes to develop strategies that get results."

After GEO

"I am a digital marketing consultant based in Ho Chi Minh City. Over four years, I have helped 18 small and mid-size businesses in Southeast Asia grow organic search traffic by an average of 45 percent - without paid advertising."

What just happened

The before version has no data, no location, no verifiable claim. The after version has all three. An AI can quote the after version and point to it. It cannot quote the before version - there is nothing to verify.

08 Recap

Key Takeaways

  • Search is shifting from ranking to citation AI tools answer questions directly. Your job is no longer to appear on a list. It is to be the source the answer quotes. That is a different game with different rules.
  • E-E-A-T is the signal set AI trusts Experience, Expertise, Authority, and Trust are not abstract values - they are signals you build into content with specific stories, real data, credible citations, and a clear author identity.
  • Write in answerable units of 80-120 words Each paragraph should be a standalone answer. If an AI read it aloud in response to a question, would it make sense? If yes, it is quotable. If not, tighten it.
  • Vague claims are invisible to AI "We help companies grow" is invisible. "We helped 12 teams reduce manual reporting time by 40 percent" is citable. The rewrite is not about length - it is about verifiability.
  • Your existing content is the fastest win You do not need to start from zero. Find your best pages, run them through the GEO rewrite prompt, and update them. One piece this week. Build the habit from there.

GEO Audit

Use this to diagnose any page before you rewrite it
Analyze this content for GEO performance. Identify: (1) which E-E-A-T signals are present and which are missing, (2) whether the content is written in answerable units of 80-120 words, (3) whether claims are specific and verifiable or vague and general, and (4) whether any credible sources are cited. Give me a short verdict for each and a priority list of what to fix first. Here is the content: [PASTE YOUR CONTENT]

Write GEO-First From Scratch

Use this when starting a new piece of content
Write a [blog post / LinkedIn post / website bio / product description] about [TOPIC]. Use GEO best practices: write in answerable units of 80-120 words each, include specific examples and data where possible, build in E-E-A-T signals, and make every paragraph quotable as a standalone answer. The target audience is [DESCRIBE YOUR AUDIENCE]. The key point I want to make is [YOUR MAIN IDEA].

Find Citable Sources

Use this to find credible sources that strengthen your content
I am writing content about [TOPIC]. I need 3-5 credible, citable sources that AI models would treat as high-authority. Prioritize academic research, published studies from universities, and data from established research organizations. For each source, give me the organization, a one-sentence description of what the research shows, and why it strengthens the E-E-A-T signals in my content.
