🔤 Words to Know

Mission 1 — Key Terms

Plain language. No jargon. No assumptions. Every concept you need to understand Mission 1 — and carry into every mission that follows.

When a term is still unclear: Ask your AI Buddy — "Explain [term] in plain language with an example from [your role or industry]." That's what it's there for.
The Big Idea
The 50/50 Era
Core Concept
The current moment in which half of every professional's work is becoming AI-led. Leadership now requires two skill sets: managing people and managing AI programs. Most leaders have only been trained for one half.
In practice: A marketing manager in the 50/50 era doesn't just manage the team writing content — she also leads the AI program that drafts, tests, and distributes it.
The AI Officer
Core Concept
A person who can identify where AI should create value in an organization, design AI programs around real business problems, and lead the people and AI working together to deliver results. It is a capability, not a job title — anyone can develop it.
In practice: The AI Officer is not the person who knows the most tools. It's the person who walks into any room, looks at any process, and knows where AI fits and how to deliver ROI from it.
The Other 50%
Core Concept
The AI half of leadership — designing AI programs, making data decisions, building logic, owning workflows, and measuring ROI. This is the half that most leaders have never been trained to do.
Understanding AI
Narrow AI
AI Type
AI that is very good at specific tasks but cannot think broadly. It can read an X-ray, write a report, or win at chess — but only what it was built to do. This is the AI you are working with today (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini).
In practice: ChatGPT can write a brilliant email but has no idea who you are, what your company does, or what happened in your last meeting — unless you tell it.
AGI — Artificial General Intelligence
AI Type
AI that can reason like a human across any domain — not just specific tasks. Estimated to arrive sometime between 2030 and 2040. Still hypothetical. We are not there yet.
LLM — Large Language Model
Technical
A pattern prediction machine trained on a massive amount of text to guess what comes next in a sentence. ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are all LLMs. Each LLM can contain multiple models — GPT-4o is a model within ChatGPT, Opus 4.6 is a model within Claude.
LLarge — trained on enormous amounts of text data LLanguage — works with text, not images or video alone MModel — a trained system that makes predictions
Hallucination
Risk
When AI makes something up and sounds completely confident about it. It is not lying — it is predicting. The pattern it saw in training suggested this answer, even if the answer is wrong. This is why the human stays in the loop.
In practice: Ask AI for a statistic without checking the source. It may give you a number that sounds plausible but is completely fabricated. Always verify facts that matter.
Context Window
Technical
The amount of text an AI model can "see" at one time — the content of your current conversation plus anything you paste in. Outside the context window, AI has no memory. Every new session starts from zero.
In practice: If you told Claude something important last week in a different chat, it has no idea. You own the continuity — you must bring the context back every time.
AI Kingdoms
Framework
The three major AI providers, each with a different data advantage. ChatGPT (Microsoft/OpenAI) is the all-rounder. Gemini (Google) is the producer with visual dominance. Claude (Anthropic) is the thoughtful pro, best at writing and coding. To choose the right AI, follow the money behind the data.
Frameworks & Techniques
RACE
Framework
Your starter framework for any structured AI task. A four-part structure that forces you to organize your thinking before you hand the task to AI.
RRole — Who is the AI acting as? Give it an identity. AAction — What should it do? Be specific. CContext — What's the situation, the data, the constraints? EExample — What does good look like? Show it the finish line.
The rule: You don't write the prompt. You organize what you know. RACE is how you organize it.
The AI Interview
Technique
The master technique for getting professional AI output. Instead of writing the full prompt yourself, you say: "I need help with [task]. Before you start, ask me 3–4 questions." AI finds gaps in your thinking you didn't know were there.
The progression: Bare prompt → RACE framework → AI Interview. Conversation wins every time.
Prompt
Basics
The instruction you give to an AI tool. A prompt can be one sentence or several paragraphs. The quality of your output is directly tied to the quality of your prompt — which is why the AI Officer focuses on frameworks, not guessing.
Framework
Basics
A structured way of organizing your thinking before giving it to AI. A framework does the heavy lifting of organizing your context, so AI can do the heavy lifting of generating useful output.
In practice: RACE is your first framework. You'll learn more in Mission 3. The goal is never to memorize the framework — it's to build the habit of organizing before you prompt.
The AI Officer Operating System
The 6 Beliefs
Mindset
The personal operating system of an AI Officer — six beliefs that guide every decision you make about AI.
1Accuracy follows clarity 2AI is like a Buddy that learns you 3Frameworks turn chaos into clarity 4Information is the new creativity 5Curiosity drives mastery 6AI needs a human in the loop
The 5 Daily Moves
Practice
The five things an AI Officer does every day to build and run AI programs.
1Find problems worth solving — where does AI create real ROI? 2Become a data detective — find the chaos before AI amplifies it 3Build or buy — know when to create vs. when to use an existing tool 4Orchestrate AI — sequence the workflow and connect the parts 5Plan like a pro — define goals, data, tools, checkpoints, and metrics
Human in the Loop
Governance
The principle that AI always needs a human to set direction, catch errors, and make judgment calls. AI can scale knowledge and process — it cannot set meaning, define ethics, or replace human judgment. The AI Officer is the human in the loop for AI programs.
ROI — Return on Investment
Business
In AI programs, ROI typically falls into four types: time saved, cost reduced, quality improved, and speed increased. At least one type should be quantified before any AI program begins — this is what separates an AI Officer from someone just experimenting with tools.
