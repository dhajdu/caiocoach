03 Context

Search Has Changed Forever

For 20 years, being findable meant ranking on Google. That era is over.

18%
Google's share of content discovery. The other 82% happens on TikTok, Instagram, Amazon, LinkedIn, AI answer engines, and niche community platforms.

Your audience does not stay in one place. They find things where they already are - in their feed, in a search on a specific platform, in an AI answer. If your content only lives in one place, most of your audience never sees it.

This is not just a marketing problem. It is a visibility problem. If you want to be recognized as someone who leads AI, people need to be able to find you on the platforms where they actually spend time.

Key Insight

Visibility is how AI Officers build credibility. Not by being on every platform yourself - by building a system that puts your thinking there automatically.

04 Concept

The Idea Is the Engine

Most people use AI backwards. They open ChatGPT and type: "write me a LinkedIn post about AI." They get something. It sounds fine. They publish it. Three months later they wonder why nothing is working.

The problem is not the tool. The problem is the order.

AI without your idea

You get the average of everything on the internet. Generic. Safe. Sounds like everyone else. No point of view. No reason for anyone to engage.

AI with your idea

You get content that only you could have written. Specific. Argued. Your experience, your data, your audience. Something worth reading.

AI is the amplifier. You are the signal. Without a strong signal, amplification just makes noise louder.

Key Insight

The time investment is not in the writing - it is in the idea. Ten minutes developing a sharp angle saves you hours of editing generic output.

05 Exercise

Try This Now

Everyone does this at the same time. Take two minutes.

Do this right now

Open ChatGPT or Claude. Start a new conversation. Type exactly this: "I have an idea. Ask me questions to make it sharper." Then tell it one thing you actually believe - something from your work, your industry, or something your team keeps getting wrong. Let it ask you questions for two minutes.

Do not wait for the perfect idea. It can be rough - something you explained to a colleague recently, something you noticed this week, something you keep saying in meetings. Just say it.

When the trainer says stop, post what you got in the chat.

What to notice

When AI asks you questions, you start answering things you did not know you knew. That is the system working. The questions surface your real expertise faster than any blank page.

06 System

Build Your Idea Project

Instead of starting from scratch every time, build a permanent home for your ideas. One setup. Endless content.

Create a Project in ChatGPT or Claude. In the instructions, include four things:

Your voice
How you actually sound

Direct? Warm? Blunt? Describe the tone and style you want the AI to match.

Your audience
Exactly who you are talking to

Role, ambition, problem they are trying to solve. Not "professionals" - be specific.

Your topics
What you want to be known for

Two or three areas where you have a real point of view. Your territory.

Your data
Sources you trust

Client results, academic papers, reports you can quote. Real numbers beat opinions.

Once the Project is set up, drop any rough idea in and ask it to help you develop it. It knows your voice, your audience, and what matters to you. The output is yours from the start.

The most valuable data source

Academic papers and client results beat internet articles every time. Anyone can quote a stat they found on a blog. When you cite real research or your own verified client data, your content is credible - and harder to dismiss. Use Perplexity Labs to find papers fast. Ask it for "academic research on [your topic]" and check the sources it returns.

07 Distribution

From Idea to Every Platform

Once your idea is developed and your angle is sharp, multi-platform generation is almost instant.

One developed idea can become all of these - in under 15 minutes:

1

Blog post (business + personal versions)

Long-form content optimized for search, including FAQ snippets that answer questions AI search engines are indexing.

2

LinkedIn thread or post

Professional, first-person, conversational. Built for the feed format and the audience that discovers people on LinkedIn.

3

Instagram carousel

Short, visual, punchy. Broken into slides with a hook, the core idea, and a call to action.

4

TikTok or short video script

Hook in the first three seconds. 60-90 seconds total. Written the way people actually talk - not read.

5

Email and localized versions

Direct outreach or newsletter format. Plus versions in other languages if your audience is multilingual.

Key Insight

The 15 minutes only works because you did the 10 minutes of idea development first. Skipping the idea development does not save time - it creates work. Generic output takes hours to fix.

08 Decision Guide

Projects vs. Apps: When to Use Each

You will hear about a lot of tools. Here is a simple decision guide.

Use a Project (LLM)

When you are developing, revising, or thinking through an idea. Projects are faster for iterating, more flexible for exploration, and better when you are still figuring out the angle. Use ChatGPT or Claude Projects.

Use an App

When you have a good idea and need to distribute it consistently, week after week, at scale. Apps add team workflows, ambassador sharing, analytics, and consistent output formats. You cannot automate a Project. You can automate an app.

One decision test: does this tool do something your LLM genuinely cannot do by itself? Team sharing with tracked links. Mobile capture that feeds a workflow. Consistent output format across channels. If yes, the app is worth it. If no, save the money and build it in your Project.

Choose your model intentionally

Do not run on auto. If you need something fast and simple, use the default model. If you need deep research or complex reasoning, choose a model that thinks longer - it will take more time but produce far better output. In Perplexity, the Labs model takes 5-10 minutes and gives you a full, sourced report. That is not a bug. That is the trade-off.

10 Recap

Key Takeaways

  • Search is fragmented. You need to be everywhere. Google is 18% of discovery. Your audience finds things on TikTok, LinkedIn, AI answers, and niche platforms. A single-channel content strategy is invisible to most of your audience.
  • Your idea is the investment. AI is the distribution. Ten minutes developing a sharp angle is worth more than an hour generating generic content. AI without your point of view produces the average of the internet. AI with your point of view produces content that stands out.
  • Build an Idea Project. Use it every week. Set up a Project with your voice, audience, topics, and data sources. Set it up once. Use it to develop every idea you publish from now on.
  • Projects for thinking. Apps for distributing. Use your LLM Project when you are developing. Use a purpose-built app when you need team workflow, tracking, and consistent multi-platform output at scale.
  • Visibility builds the AI Officer reputation. When you build this system for yourself, you start appearing where decision-makers find people. When you hand it to your team, you are leading AI - not just using it.
