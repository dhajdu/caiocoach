Slide 1 1 minute
AI Browsers: Work Smarter Without the Copy-Paste Tax
Your AI is blind to your browser.
AI browsers fix that.
Generative AI Essentials - Micro Session
Welcome. Quick question - how much time did you spend today copying text from a website into ChatGPT or Gemini? That time has a name: the copy-paste tax. Every time you jump between your browser and your AI, you pay it. AI browsers are the first real attempt to eliminate it. In the next 30 minutes, you are going to understand exactly what changed, try it live, and walk out with a new tool that you can make your default browser today.
Slide 2 3 minutes
The Gap Nobody Talks About
Your browser sees everything.
Your AI sees nothing.
AI browsers collapse that gap.
Think about it this way. Your browser has your full history, every site you have visited, every tab you have open right now. Your AI tool has none of that. It only knows what you paste into the text box. So every time you want to use AI on something you found online, you have to copy it, switch tools, paste it, prompt it, copy the result, and then go back. That round trip is the copy-paste tax. It happens 10 to 30 times a day for most knowledge workers. AI browsers like Perplexity Comet and Atlas from ChatGPT put the AI directly inside the browser so that tax disappears.
Slide 3 3 minutes
What AI Browsers Actually Unlock
Sidecar assistant - AI lives on the page with you
Page context - no copy-paste needed
Agent mode - clicks and acts on your behalf
Background assistant - runs tasks while you work
Here are the four things AI browsers unlock that browser plug-ins cannot fully replicate. First, the sidecar assistant - a chat panel that lives inside your browser, always open, always seeing what you see. Second, page context - when the AI can see the page you are on, you do not have to explain it. You just ask a question. Third, agent mode - this is the big one. The AI can actually click buttons, fill forms, and navigate websites on your behalf. It acts like automated test software running inside your account. Fourth, background assistant - you can schedule tasks and the browser runs them while you are doing something else. Most tools today do the first two well. Agent mode is early but coming fast.
Slide 4 7 minutes (including exercise)
Before Challenge - The Old Way (3 min)
Find prices and times for 4 flights.
Use your normal browser.
3 minutes. Go.
Everybody do this now. I am going to paste 4 flights into the chat. Try to find the prices, airlines, and departure times for all four using whatever browser you normally use. Set a timer. This is not a trick - it is just something you might actually need to do. Notice how many tabs you open, how many times you copy something into a notepad, how long it takes before you have all four answers in one place. That experience is the problem AI browsers are solving. When your 3 minutes are up, drop what you found in the chat.
Slide 5 12 minutes (including exercise)
The New Way - Perplexity Comet (5 min)
1. Open comet.perplexity.ai
2. Start with a prompt - not a search
3. Watch it work
Now do the same task in Comet. If you have not downloaded it yet, go to perplexity.com/comet - it is free with a Google account. Create a new tab and instead of typing the flights into a search bar, write a prompt. Something like: here are 4 flights I need to book, find me the best prices on Vietnam Airlines if possible. Then watch what happens. Comet will open tabs, search flight sites, and pull results back - all inside the browser, all without you copying anything. It is using the browser the same way automated test software does - it finds page elements, clicks them, and reads the results. This is not magic. It is a different kind of tool.
Slide 6 2 minutes
Comet vs Atlas - Quick Comparison
Comet: real live search, more accurate
Atlas: faster but uses estimated data
Both Comet from Perplexity and Atlas from ChatGPT are built on Chromium - Google's open-source browser framework. You can log in with your Google account, sync your history, and use them as a full Chrome replacement. The difference I found in real use: Comet uses Perplexity's live search database, which is still the best AI search engine available. When I asked it to find flights, it went to actual flight sites and returned real prices. Atlas was faster but it was guessing - it gave me a $63 Vietnam to KL fare that was not real. Accuracy matters more than speed when you are making decisions. Start with Comet.
Slide 7 2 minutes
The Privacy Trade-Off
Your browser knows more about you than any app.
Now it is also your AI.
Know what you are sharing.
This is the part that matters for leaders. When you use an AI browser, you are giving the AI visibility into everything your browser sees - every tab, every search, every page. If you are using it for personal research or planning a trip, the trade-off is clear and probably worth it. If you are using it for sensitive client work, internal strategy, or confidential communications - pause and think. The data you hand to your browser's AI goes somewhere. Know your organization's policy. And remember: Google Chrome has been doing this quietly for years. At least with AI browsers, the value exchange is obvious.
Slide 8 2 minutes
Your Challenge
Replace one real research task with an AI browser this week.
Submit your prompt and what you found to your AI Buddy.
Here is your challenge. Pick one task you do every week that involves looking things up - competitive research, news monitoring, finding pricing, pulling industry data, sourcing suppliers, anything with multiple tabs and copy-pasting. Do it in Comet instead. Write the prompt you used, describe what the AI did, and note where it helped versus where it got things wrong. Submit that to your AI Buddy in AIO Labs. Your AI Buddy will evaluate the quality of your prompt and how well you identified the leadership value of reducing research friction. This is one credit toward your Generative AI Essentials certification.
Slide 9 1 minute
The Big Idea
The copy-paste tax is real.
AI browsers are the first tool that charges it back.
Start with the research tasks. Build from there.
Most people using AI today are doing it inefficiently. They find something, copy it, switch tools, paste it in, get an answer, copy it back. That is not AI-powered work. That is two workflows duct-taped together. AI browsers start to change that. Start with the tasks where you do the most tab-switching and the most copying. That is where you will feel the difference fastest. See you at the next session.
